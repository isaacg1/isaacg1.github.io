Today, I'm tracing back my line of academic advisors (PhD advisor to PhD advisor) as far as I can, using the [Mathematics Genealogy](https://www.mathgenealogy.org). The longest-ago person in their database appears to be [Ibn Sina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avicenna), also known as Avicenna, a Persian scholar who lived 980-1037, and a few guesses as to his potential advisors. Ibn Sina is in my line of academic advisors, so I'm tracing the path from him to me (or me back to him).

I am listing PhD advisors, or if a person has no PhD the advisor of the last graduate degree they received, or of the nearest dissertation equivalent. I also list the university or nearest equivalent where degree was achieved. Many people had multiple advisors, but to avoid an exponential explosion of advisors, I'm only tracking one advisor, chosen to reach Ibn Sina. There are many paths that reach him, and I chose arbitrarily amongst those paths. For each person, I list their name, university, degree year, years of birth and death, and a link to their Mathematics Genealogy page. I also include a short version of their name if they are better known under that short version.
